Chapter 1: How LLMs Function – Networking Perspective

Page 4 – Networking Perspective

Large Language Models (LLMs) operate like distributed networks. At their core, they are collections of interconnected nodes (neurons) that process tokens — the smallest units of text. Each token passes through layers of the network, where mathematical operations transform it into meaningful representations.

From a networking perspective, LLMs resemble communication systems. Input text is encoded into vectors, transmitted through multiple layers, and decoded back into human‑readable language. This flow mirrors how data packets travel across computer networks.

The transformer architecture introduced attention mechanisms, allowing the model to focus on relevant parts of the input. This is similar to how routers prioritize certain packets in a network, ensuring efficient and accurate delivery of information.

Practical Exercise

  • Think of a sentence as a series of data packets.
  • Imagine each word being routed through a network of nodes.
  • Observe how the LLM reconstructs meaning by attending to key words.

By viewing LLMs through a networking lens, beginners can better understand how information flows inside these systems. This analogy makes the abstract mathematics more relatable.
