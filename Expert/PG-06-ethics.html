Chapter 3: Ethical Considerations – Responsible AI Use

Page 6 – Ethics

As LLMs become embedded in professional and personal workflows, ethical considerations must guide their use. Responsible AI practice ensures fairness, transparency, and accountability while minimizing harm.

Key Ethical Principles

  • Transparency – Clearly communicate when outputs are AI‑generated.
  • Fairness – Avoid biased prompts and outputs that disadvantage groups.
  • Accountability – Ensure human oversight in critical decisions.
  • Privacy – Protect sensitive data and avoid unnecessary disclosure.

Ethical use of LLMs is not optional. It is a responsibility that ensures trust and safeguards both individuals and organizations.

Best Practices

  • Audit outputs regularly for bias or inaccuracies.
  • Establish clear policies for AI use in your organization.
  • Educate teams on ethical prompt design.
  • Maintain transparency with stakeholders about AI involvement.

Practical Exercise

  • Write a prompt that could unintentionally introduce bias.
  • Revise the prompt to remove bias and ensure fairness.
  • Compare the outputs and reflect on the ethical implications.

By practicing ethical considerations, expert users ensure that LLMs are used responsibly, fostering trust and long‑term sustainability in AI adoption.
