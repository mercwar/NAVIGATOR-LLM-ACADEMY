Chapter 6: Transparency – Making AI Understandable

Page 3 – Transparency

Transparency ensures that AI systems are not “black boxes.” By making processes understandable, users and stakeholders can trust and evaluate AI outputs.

Why Transparency Matters

  • Trust – Clear explanations build confidence in AI decisions.
  • Accountability – Transparent systems allow responsibility to be assigned.
  • Learning – Users can understand how outputs are generated.
  • Compliance – Regulations often require explainability.

Best Practices

  • Provide documentation of model design and training data sources.
  • Offer user‑friendly explanations of outputs.
  • Use visualization tools to show decision pathways.
  • Disclose limitations and uncertainties openly.

Practical Exercise

  • Choose one AI workflow you use.
  • Write a plain‑language explanation of how it produces outputs.
  • Identify areas where transparency could be improved.
  • Share your explanation with a non‑technical peer for feedback.

By embedding transparency, expert users make AI systems understandable, accountable, and more widely accepted.
