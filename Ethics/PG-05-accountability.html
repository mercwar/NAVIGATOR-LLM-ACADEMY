Chapter 6: Accountability – Assigning Responsibility in AI

Page 5 – Accountability

Accountability ensures that responsibility for AI decisions and outcomes is clear. Without accountability, errors or harms may go unaddressed, undermining trust in AI systems.

Why Accountability Matters

  • Responsibility – Assigns ownership for AI actions and decisions.
  • Trust – Users feel confident when accountability structures exist.
  • Compliance – Regulations often require clear accountability frameworks.
  • Ethics – Ensures that AI use aligns with moral obligations.

Best Practices

  • Define clear roles for AI oversight within organizations.
  • Document decision‑making processes and responsible parties.
  • Establish escalation protocols for errors or harms.
  • Regularly review accountability structures for effectiveness.

Practical Exercise

  • Choose one AI workflow in your organization.
  • Identify who is accountable for its outputs.
  • Design a clear escalation path for errors.
  • Document accountability measures for transparency.

By embedding accountability, expert users ensure that AI systems operate responsibly, fostering trust and compliance across diverse contexts.
