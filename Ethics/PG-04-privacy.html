Chapter 6: Privacy – Protecting User Data

Page 4 – Privacy

Privacy is a cornerstone of ethical AI practice. Protecting user data ensures trust, compliance, and respect for individual rights in every AI interaction.

Why Privacy Matters

  • Trust – Users must feel confident their information is secure.
  • Compliance – Regulations like GDPR and HIPAA mandate strict protections.
  • Ethics – Respecting privacy is a moral obligation, not just a legal one.
  • Risk Reduction – Strong privacy safeguards prevent misuse and breaches.

Best Practices

  • Minimize data collection to only what is necessary.
  • Encrypt sensitive information both in transit and at rest.
  • Implement strict access controls and audit logs.
  • Regularly review and update privacy policies.

Practical Exercise

  • Review one AI workflow you use.
  • Identify what personal data is collected.
  • Design safeguards to minimize and protect that data.
  • Document your privacy strategy for accountability.

By prioritizing privacy, expert users ensure that AI systems respect individuals, comply with regulations, and maintain long‑term trust.
