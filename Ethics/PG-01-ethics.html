Chapter 6: Ethical Dimensions – Responsible AI Practice

Page 1 – Introduction to Ethics

As AI systems become more powerful and widespread, ethical considerations are essential. Responsible practice ensures that LLMs are used fairly, transparently, and in ways that benefit society.

Core Ethical Principles

  • Fairness – Avoid bias and ensure equitable outcomes.
  • Transparency – Make AI processes understandable and accountable.
  • Privacy – Protect sensitive data and respect user rights.
  • Accountability – Ensure responsibility for AI decisions and outputs.

Ethical dimensions guide the responsible integration of AI, preventing misuse and fostering trust between humans and technology.

Best Practices

  • Audit AI systems regularly for bias and fairness.
  • Disclose when outputs are AI‑generated.
  • Implement strong data protection measures.
  • Establish clear accountability structures for AI use.

Practical Exercise

  • Review one AI workflow you use.
  • Identify potential ethical risks (bias, privacy, transparency).
  • Design safeguards to address these risks.
  • Document your ethical framework for future reference.

By embedding ethics into practice, expert users ensure that AI systems contribute positively to society while minimizing harm.
