Chapter 6: Bias – Identifying and Reducing Bias in AI

Page 2 – Bias

Bias in AI systems can lead to unfair, inaccurate, or harmful outcomes. Identifying and reducing bias is essential to ensure ethical and equitable AI practice.

Why Bias Matters

  • Fairness – Prevents discrimination against individuals or groups.
  • Accuracy – Reduces distortions in AI outputs.
  • Trust – Builds confidence in AI systems among diverse users.
  • Compliance – Meets legal and regulatory standards for fairness.

Best Practices

  • Audit datasets for representation and diversity.
  • Use bias detection tools during model training.
  • Engage diverse stakeholders in testing and evaluation.
  • Continuously monitor outputs for signs of bias.

Practical Exercise

  • Choose one dataset you use.
  • Analyze it for representation gaps.
  • Propose strategies to reduce bias in training.
  • Document improvements and share with peers.

By addressing bias, expert users ensure that AI systems deliver fair, accurate, and trustworthy outcomes across diverse contexts.
