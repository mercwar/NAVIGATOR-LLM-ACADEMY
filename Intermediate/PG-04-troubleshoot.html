Chapter 2: Troubleshooting Common Issues

Page 4 – Troubleshooting

Even experienced users encounter problems when working with Large Language Models (LLMs). Common issues include vague responses, loss of context, or outputs that don’t match the intended format. Troubleshooting these problems is part of becoming an effective LLM user.

Frequent Issues

  • Vague Answers – Caused by unclear prompts. Solution: add context and constraints.
  • Loss of Context – Happens in long conversations. Solution: restate key information.
  • Incorrect Formatting – Output doesn’t match expectations. Solution: specify format (e.g., bullet points).
  • Hallucinations – Model invents facts. Solution: verify with reliable sources.

By recognizing these issues early, you can adjust your prompts and workflows to maintain accuracy and efficiency.

Practical Exercise

  • Give your LLM a vague prompt (e.g., “Tell me something interesting”).
  • Observe the response and note its weaknesses.
  • Refine the prompt with context (e.g., “Tell me three interesting facts about space exploration”).
  • Compare the improved output to the original.

Troubleshooting is a skill that grows with practice. By experimenting with different approaches, you’ll learn how to guide the model toward reliable and useful results.
