Chapter 2: Prompt Engineering Basics

Page 1 – Introduction to Prompt Engineering

Prompt engineering is the art of crafting inputs that guide Large Language Models (LLMs) toward producing useful, accurate, and creative outputs. While beginners may simply type questions, intermediate users learn to design prompts with structure and clarity.

A well‑engineered prompt provides context, specifies the desired format, and sets boundaries for the model’s response. This reduces errors and improves the quality of results.

Key Principles

  • Clarity – State exactly what you want.
  • Context – Provide background information or examples.
  • Constraints – Limit the scope (e.g., word count, style).
  • Iteration – Refine prompts based on model responses.

Prompt engineering is a skill that grows with practice. By experimenting with different phrasing and formats, you’ll learn how to unlock the full potential of LLMs.

Practical Exercise

  • Ask your LLM to “summarize the history of the internet in 3 bullet points.”
  • Compare the result to a vague prompt like “tell me about the internet.”
  • Notice how clarity and constraints improve the output.
